**DETAILED FINDINGS**  
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  

**1. ABS System Disabled**  
- **What it is:** The ABS helps prevent your wheels from locking up during hard braking, allowing you to maintain control.  
- **Why it happened:** This can be caused by faulty sensors, damaged wires, or a malfunction in the ABS control module.  
- **What it affects:** Without a functioning ABS, your braking performance is compromised, which can be dangerous.  
- **What needs to be done:** We recommend inspecting the sensors and wiring, and possibly testing the ABS control module for issues.  

**2. Tire Pressure Monitor Sensor Battery Low**  
- **What it is:** This sensor monitors the air pressure in your tires, alerting you if they are under-inflated.  
- **Why it happened:** The batteries in these sensors can wear out over time (usually lasting 5-10 years).  
- **What it affects:** Low tire pressure can lead to poor fuel efficiency and increased tire wear.  
- **What needs to be done:** Replace the sensor(s) with low batteries and check tire pressures to ensure they are at the recommended level (usually around 35 PSI).  

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━  
**RECOMMENDED ACTIONS**

**Priority 1 (Do First):**  
- Inspect all ABS speed sensors for damage or disconnection.  
- Check wiring for corrosion or damage.  
- If no issues are found, test the ABS control module for faults.  

**Priority 2 (Do Soon):**  
- Replace any TPMS sensor(s) with low battery.  
- Adjust tire pressures to the recommended level.  
- Recalibrate the TPMS system after replacing sensors to ensure they work correctly.  

**Maintenance Tips:**  
- Regularly check your tire pressure, ideally once a month.  
- Schedule routine inspections for your ABS system and tire pressure sensors.
